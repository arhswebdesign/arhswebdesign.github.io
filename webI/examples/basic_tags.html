Rare and endangered flowers of Cape Cod

Introduction

There are many flowers on Cape Cod that are in danger of becoming extinct.

The flowers

1.  Bog Candles (aka Leafy White Orchis)

Latin name:

Plantathera dilata

Blooms:

June-July

Habitat:

Spring Seeps

MESA* Listing:

Threatened
* Massachussets Endangered Species Act,
http://www.mass.gov/eea/agencies/dfg/dfw/natural-heritage/species-information-and-conservation/mesa-list/list-of-rare-species-in-massachusetts.html#MESAplant

Description:

A first-time sighting of the bog-candle in full flower leaves the beholder impressed by its beauty and size. Decorating a three to four foot high, leafy spike are dozens of snow white flowers. Their outstretched lateral sepals give the flowers a curious look resembling little Caspar the Friendly Ghosts or flying nuns. These odd blossoms are a delight from an olfactory aspect as well, exuding the spicy fragrance of cloves. This feature gives it the name "scent bottle' in the northern Maritimes.

There is but one colony of bog candles on Cape Cod, an historic site in East Sandwich which has been watched closely by botanists for many years. The habitat is thick muck formed by spring seeps from a clay bank. There may be a buffering agent or calcium depost, (perhaps from shells in an ancient Indian midden) to explain how this plant became established in so acidic and otherwise hostile an environment as Cape Cod.

This species is rare throughout the state, with only four recorded sites presently known in Massachusetts*.

*quoted from DiGregorio, Mario J. and Jeff Walker. 1989. Cape Cod Wildflowers.
